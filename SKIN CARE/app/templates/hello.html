VITILOGA
Vitiligo is a condition where the skin loses its pigment due to the destruction of melanocytes. While there’s no definitive cure, various treatments can help restore pigmentation, or improve the skin’s appearance.
MELESEMA
Melasma is a common skin condition characterized by brown or grayish patches, often appearing on the face, caused by sun exposure, hormonal changes, or genetic factors.Sun exposure is the most significant.
ACNE
Acne treatment varies depending on its severity, skin type underlying and they classified into atrophic scars (depressions in the skin like icepick, rolling, or boxcar scars) and hypertrophic scars (raised scars).
ALOPECIA-AREATA
Alopecia Areata is an autoimmune condition in which the immune system mistakenly attacks hair follicles, leading to sudden and often patchy hair loss on the scalp, face, or other parts of the body. While the exact cause is not fully understood, it is believed to result from a combination of genetic predisposition.
DRY
Dry skin, also known as xerosis, occurs when the skin lacks adequate moisture and can result in flakiness, itchiness, and tightness. To treat and prevent it, moisturizing regularly with thick, fragrance-free creams or ointments, such as those containing ceramides or petroleum jelly, is essential.
WARTS
Warts are small, rough growths caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV), and their treatment depends on their size, location, and persistence. Over-the-counter remedies, such as salicylic acid in the form of gels or patches, can help dissolve the wart gradually, especially when applied after soaking the wart in warm water.
